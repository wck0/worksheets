Intuitively, a sequence is a list of numbers. More formally, a sequence \(\{a_n\}_{n \geq m}\) is the list of numbers \(a_m, a_{m+1}, a_{m+2}, \dots\text{.}\)

Example 1.1.1. A Sequence.

The sequence \(\{a_n\}_{n \geq 2}\) where \(a_n = 3n-4\) is the sequnce below:

\begin{equation*} 2, 5, 8, 11, 14, 17, \dots \end{equation*}

We might more often describe that sequence by declaring that \(a_n = 3n-4\) for \(n \geq 2\)
