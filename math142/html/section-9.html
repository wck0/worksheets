Section 4.1 \(\displaystyle \int_0^\pi \sin(x) \; dx\)

Here you will compute \(\displaystyle \int_0^\pi \sin(x) \; dx\) using the definition of the Riemann integral.

Activity 4.1.1.

Consider \(\displaystyle \int_{0}^\pi \sin(x) \; dx\text{.}\)

(a)

Check that in this case \(x_i = i \Delta x\text{.}\) (For what we are doing, we want everything in terms of \(\Delta x\text{.}\))

(b)

Write the Riemann sum \(\displaystyle \sum_{i=1}^n \sin(x_i) \Delta x\text{.}\)

(c)

(d)

Write down the difference of angles formula for the cosine function. (Look this up if you have to.)

\begin{equation*} \cos(A-B) = \end{equation*}
(e)

Use the above to simplify \(\cos(A-B) - \cos(A+B)\text{.}\)

(f)

Replace \(A-B\) with \(a\) and \(A+B\) with \(b\) and simplify.

(g)

Check that you have \(\displaystyle \cos(b) - \cos(a) = 2 \sin \left(\frac{a+b}{2}\right) \sin\left(\frac{a-b}{2}\right)\text{.}\)

(h)

Plug in \(\displaystyle a = \frac{2i+1}{2} \Delta x\) and \(\displaystyle b = \frac{2i-1}{2}\Delta x\) and simplify.

(i)

You should have an equation involving \(\sin(i \Delta x)\text{.}\) Solve your equation for this term.

(j)

Use your last result to rewrite the Riemann sum under investigation: \(\displaystyle \sum_{i=1}^n \sin(x_i) \Delta x\text{.}\)

(k)

Factor out all terms which do not contain \(i\text{.}\) You should be left with a difference of two cosine terms in the summation.

(l)

Evaluate that sum. (Hint: It is a telescoping sum. Write out some of the terms to see this. The whole point of all of the previous work was to get our sum to look like a telescoping sum.)

(m)

Recall that \(\displaystyle \int_0^\pi \sin(x) \;dx = \lim_{n \to \infty} \sum_{i=1}^n \sin(x_i) \Delta x\text{.}\)

(n)

If \(n \to \infty\text{,}\) what happens to \(\Delta x\text{?}\)

(o)

Express \(\displaystyle \int_0^\pi \sin(x) \; dx\) as a limit involving \(\Delta x\text{.}\) That is, replace \(\displaystyle \lim_{n \to \infty}\) with \(\displaystyle \lim_{\Delta x \to c}\) for the appropriate value of \(c\text{.}\)

(p)

Evaluate that limit.

(q)

What is \(\displaystyle \int_0^\pi \sin(x) \; dx\text{?}\)

(r)

Draw a picture illustrating what this definite integral represents.
