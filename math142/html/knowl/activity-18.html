Activity 3.1.1.

Let \(f(x) = x+1\) and \([a,b] = [-1,2]\text{.}\)

(a)
Make a sketch which indicates what \(\displaystyle\int_{-1}^2 f(x) \; dx\) represents.
(b)

Determine \(\Delta x\)

(c)

Determine \(x_i\text{.}\)

(d)

Determine the Riemann sum \(\displaystyle\sum_{i=1}^n f(x_i) \Delta x\text{.}\) Simplify.

(e)

If you didn't already, make use of the fact that \(\displaystyle \sum_{i=1}^n i = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}\) and \(\displaystyle \sum_{i=1}^n 1 = n\) to further simplify your Riemann sum.

(f)

Compute \(\displaystyle\lim_{n \to \infty} \sum_{i=1}^n f(x_i) \Delta x\text{.}\)

(g)

Compute \(\displaystyle\int_{-1}^2 x+1 \; dx\text{.}\)

(h)

Compare your results with your sketch. Does it make sense? Explain.
