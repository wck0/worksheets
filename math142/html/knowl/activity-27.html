Consider the antiderivative \(\displaystyle \int \cos(5x)\;dx\)

(a)

Check that if \(u = 5x\) and \(f(x) = \cos(x)\text{,}\) then the integrand is \(f(u)\)

(b)

With \(u = 5x\text{,}\) determine \(du\text{,}\) and solve that equation for \(dx\text{.}\)

(c)

Use your work to rewrite the original antiderivative in terms of \(u\text{.}\) Take care that there are not any \(x\)'s at all in your expression.

(d)

The new antiderivative in terms of \(u\)'s should be simpler. Compute it.

Take your result, and rewrite it in terms of \(x\)'s again.
