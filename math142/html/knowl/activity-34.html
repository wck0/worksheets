Activity 7.2.1.

Determine the area of the regions enclosed by the curves listed below. Draw a picture for each one.

(a)
\(y=x^2\text{,}\) \(y=8-x^2\)
(b)
\(y=x^2\text{,}\) \(y=8-x^2\text{,}\) in the first quadrant. (Hint: use symmetry.)
(c)
\(y=\sqrt{x}\text{,}\) \(y=2\sqrt{x}\text{,}\) \(x=4\text{,}\) \(x=9\)
(d)
\(y=\sqrt{x}\text{,}\) \(y=2\sqrt{x}\text{,}\) \(x=9\)
(e)
\(y=\sqrt{x}\text{,}\) \(y=2\sqrt{x}\text{,}\) \(x=4\) (Hint: use the last two.)
(f)
\(y=\cos(\pi x / 2)\text{,}\) \(y= 1-x^2\text{,}\) in the first quadrant. Explain how, without graphing, you could determine which of the two curves is "on top."
(g)
\(y=x\text{,}\) \(y=\sqrt{x}\text{,}\) \(y=4\) (Hint: turn your head sideways.)
(h)
\(y=\ln(x)\text{,}\) \(y=0\text{,}\) \(x=e\)
(i)
\(y=\ln(x)\text{,}\) \(y=0\text{,}\) \(x=e\text{,}\) but with your head sideways (unless that's what you did the first time, in which case do it with your head upright).
